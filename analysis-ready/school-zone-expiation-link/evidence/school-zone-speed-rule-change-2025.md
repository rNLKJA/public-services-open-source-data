# Evidence: SA school zone speed limit rule change, 2025

## The change

From **29 September 2025**, South Australia operates two school-zone speed limits simultaneously:

1. **25 km/h "when children present"** — the existing rule, unchanged, no fixed hours, triggered by flashing lights/flags/children visibly present.
2. **40 km/h time-based "school precinct" limit** — new, applies on nominated major roads near schools, **8:00-9:30am and 2:00-4:00pm on school days only** (not weekends, public holidays or school holidays). Does not replace or remove the existing 25 km/h zones on local streets.

## Rollout

- Took effect from 29 September 2025.
- Phased rollout to approximately **150 schools statewide by the end of 2026**; the Department for Infrastructure and Transport (DIT) began installing signage from late October 2025.
- First live sites: **Marryatville High School, Goodwood Primary, and St Thomas School**, switched on by November 2025.
- Signage: a "40 ahead" warning sign 60-80m before the zone, a "40 km/h school precinct" sign at the start of the zone, bidirectional time-of-operation signs at side streets, and an illuminated electronic 40 km/h sign at selected sites.

## What's not yet published

DIT's project pages describe the rollout in general terms (target ~150 schools, end of 2026) but a structured, dated, site-by-site list of which schools have gone live and when was not found as open data. That list would be needed to isolate 40 km/h precinct enforcement specifically from general 40 km/h camera enforcement (see the parent module's methodology note). Worth checking DIT's project page periodically, or requesting the list directly, as the rollout progresses.
