# SA Police use-of-force / oversight data — a documented gap

**Checked:** 6 July 2026

## Finding

No genuine open dataset for SA Police use-of-force exists as of this writing. That's documented here so the gap is explicit rather than silently absent from this repository.

## What was checked

- **SAPOL Annual Reporting Data** ([data.sa.gov.au](https://data.sa.gov.au/data/dataset/annual-reporting-data), CC BY 4.0) — the "Complaints" CSV breaks complaints down by category (staff attitude, service responsiveness, timeliness, etc.) as yearly totals from 2018-19 to 2024-25. **No use-of-force category exists in this breakdown.**
- **Office for Public Integrity (OPI)** — the SA body overseeing police complaints since the Police Ombudsman closed in September 2017 (under the Police Complaints and Discipline Act 2016). Its [2024-25 Annual Report](https://www.publicintegrity.sa.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0008/1194281/OPI-Annual-Report-2024-25.pdf) reports complaint/referral volumes and process statistics as PDF narrative, not a structured use-of-force dataset.
- **ICAC SA** — publishes reviews of the complaints *process* (e.g. a review of the Police Complaints and Discipline Act), not use-of-force statistics.
- **Audit Office of South Australia** — general SAPOL financial/agency audits exist; no use-of-force-specific performance audit was found (unlike the ANAO's federal-level "Management of the Use of Force Regime" audit, which covers a different agency entirely).
- A keyword search of data.sa.gov.au for "use of force" returns no policing-relevant results (327 hits, all agricultural/land-use/labour-force datasets — false-positive keyword matches).

## Nearest available proxy

The SAPOL "Complaints" CSV above is the closest real, open, structured dataset — but it measures complaint categories, not use-of-force incidents, and should not be relabelled as such.

## Corroborating context

An independent, non-government source states that "there is no public reporting on Taser use by police in any state or territory [in Australia], except in the ACT where minimal detail is provided" (The Conversation, 2023) — consistent with what this search found for SA specifically, and a useful sanity check that this is a known, general transparency gap rather than something specific to how this search was run.

## If this matters for your work

The next step beyond open-data search would be a direct request to SAPOL or a formal Freedom of Information application — not further web search. Worth rechecking this page periodically in case that changes.
